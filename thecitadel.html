The Citadel

My time at The Citadel was rather interesting. Upon arrival I was exposed to the military environment that shocked me, and made me question whether or not I had made the right college choice. I can truly say that my first month at The Citadel was the worst month of my life. The Citadel is not like most military schools. They have a set of guidelines that all freshmen, or "Knobs" must abide by. The rules are called the, "4th Class System." These rules require all, "Knobs" to brace at all times while in the dorms, (bracing is simply sticking one's chin in to make wrinkles under the chin while standing or walking in a rigid manner). It also requires all "Knobs" to remain silent at all times, unless in a classroom environment, in their own rooms, or if they are spoken to by an upperclassmen or military officer. It also requires "Knobs" to march everywhere in a formation. This includes PT, breakfast, lunch, dinner, assembilies, etc... Along with this, while at the dining hall, the 4th class system requires all "Knobs" to eat sitting on the edge of the chair, and to square all bites (picking up the food and putting it in your mouth in a square motion). You can start to see why this wasn't an enjoyable culture for someone to be in. After my first month of living by the 4th class system, I became so used to it, that I was no longer bothered by the stupidity of what I was doing. In fact, I began to somewhat like it. I made friends with fellow cadets, enjoyed the city of Charleston while on weekend leave, and really began to appreciate the kind of person I was becoming because of these, "stupid rules." As time progressed, and the semester began to near its end, I came to the realization that unless the Army was able to pay for more of my schooling, I wasn't going to be able to return for the next semester. In my final week at The Citadel I recieved word that I the Army was unable to pay for the next semester of my school, and so it was official... I would be returning to Maine and begining all over in the Army program on the campus of Orono.
